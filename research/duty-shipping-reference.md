# Duty and Shipping Reference Study

## 1. Introduction
In international e-commerce, the price of a product is not the only cost a buyer has to pay. Additional charges such as **international shipping fees** and **customs duties** significantly increase the final cost. These extra charges often make cross-border shopping expensive and unpredictable, especially for Indian consumers.

Understanding how shipping costs and customs duties work is important to evaluate whether a global marketplace platform can make international shopping more practical and affordable.

---

## 2. International Shipping Cost
International shipping cost is the amount paid to transport goods from one country to another. This cost depends on several factors:
* **Country of origin and destination** (Distance & Trade Route).
* **Weight and size** (Volumetric Weight vs. Actual Weight).
* **Mode of transport** (Air Courier vs. Sea Freight).
* **Shipping company** or service provider (DHL, FedEx, Aramex, Postal Unions).

For individual buyers, shipping costs are usually very high because each product is shipped separately. In many cases, the shipping cost can be almost equal to or even higher than the product price itself. This discourages users from ordering international products.

> **Key Observation:** When multiple orders are shipped together as a **bulk or consolidated shipment**, the cost per product reduces significantly. This observation forms the foundation for introducing a **group-buying mechanism** in the proposed platform.

---

## 3. Customs Duty and Import Charges
Customs duty is a tax charged by a country on goods entering its borders. In India, imported products generally attract the following charges:
1.  **Basic Customs Duty (BCD):** The primary tax on imported goods.
2.  **Social Welfare Surcharge (SWS):** Typically **10%** of the BCD value.
3.  **Integrated Goods and Services Tax (IGST):** Levied on the value of (Product + BCD + SWS).

The exact duty depends on:
* Product category (HSN Code).
* Material and usage.
* Country of origin.

Because of this complexity, buyers often do not know the final payable amount until the product reaches customs clearance, which leads to confusion and dissatisfaction.

## 6. Landing Price Estimation
The **Landing Price** is the final amount paid by the buyer after adding shipping and customs duty to the product base price.

### Example Calculation (Standard vs. Group Buy)
**Scenario:** Buying a niche mechanical keyboard from the USA.
* **Product Price:** ₹20,000

| Cost Component | Individual Purchase | Group Buy Purchase |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Product Cost** | ₹20,000 | ₹20,000 |
| **Shipping** | ₹4,000 (Standard Courier) | ₹1,200 (Consolidated) |
| **Customs Duty** | ~₹5,000 (Calculated on CIF) | ~₹4,400 (Lower Freight Base) |
| **TOTAL** | **₹29,000** | **₹25,600** |

> **Result:** The user saves **₹3,400 (approx. 12%)** on the final price, primarily due to shipping efficiencies.

## 7. Relevance to the Proposed Project
One of the biggest problems in cross-border commerce is the lack of clarity in shipping and duty costs. This study supports the need for the proposed **Global Cross-Border Marketplace Platform**, which aims to:
1.  Show **estimated landing prices** upfront.
2.  Reduce shipping costs through **group-buying**.
3.  Improve **trust and transparency**.
4.  Help users make **informed purchase decisions**.
5.  Make global products **more accessible**.

---

## 8. Conclusion
International shipping charges and customs duties play a major role in determining the affordability of cross-border products. When these costs are unclear, users hesitate to place orders.

A platform that:
1.  **Estimates landing prices** in advance, and
2.  **Enables cost-sharing** through group-buying

...can effectively reduce uncertainty, improve affordability, and encourage cross-border commerce for both Indian and global consumers.
